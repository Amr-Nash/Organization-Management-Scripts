CEO Gender-Based Termination Concerns: Evidence From Initial Severance Agreements

Felice B. Klein https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7501-8120 feliceklein@boisestate.edu, Pierre Chaigneau, and Cynthia E. DeversView all authors and affiliations

Abstract

We theorize that female candidates considering CEO roles will perceive greater termination vulnerability in such roles than their male counterparts. We further theorize that indicators of recent organizational distress will exacerbate female CEO candidatesâ perceptions of termination vulnerability, while the presence of female leaders will mitigate these concerns. To test our arguments, we examine the initial values of newly appointed female and male CEOsâ severance agreements from 2007 to 2014. Results support our arguments and begin to shed light on the factors that influence female executivesâ concerns about CEO roles and ultimately firmsâ ability to appoint female CEOs.
